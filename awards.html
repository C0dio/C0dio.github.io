Awards

Congratulations to the winners, nominated and voted for by CAMRA members from a choice of nearly 200 recognised real ale pubs and clubs in the branch area.

Vale of Glamorgan and Bridgend Awards

Vale of Glamorgan & Bridgend's Overall Winner Branch Pub
Cross Inn, Cwmfelin

This friendly multi-roomed pub with its own green-energy-run Cerddin Brewery is situated between Maesteg and Bridgend, with buses stopping at the front door and the railway station within walking distance. Excellent beer, a warm welcome, and good for the environment too!

2019
Vale of Glamorgan's Pub of the Year
The Star Inn, Wick

Gaining a reputation for excellent food with a regularly changing menu, and fine quality ale.

Refurbished a few years ago, returning the pub to its former glories, it was riginally three farm cottages. The interior has a traditional bar plus a lounge/diner and flagstones throughout.

2019
Vale of Glamorgan and Bridgend's Cider Pub of the Year
Plough & Harrow, Monknash

Renowned, welcoming 14th-century pub, originally a monastic farmhouse for Neath Abbey, with many original features remaining. Up to eight real ales available, four on handpump and the others direct from casks behind the bar, with local breweries well supported along with real cider and perry. Good home-cooked food served, and the large log fires add warmth in winter.

2019
Vale of Glamorgan and Bridgend's Club of the Year
West End Club, Barry

A welcoming atmosphere and an ever-changing range of real ales and ciders. Set on a hill overlooking Barry Old Harbour, the club welcomes visitors and hosts beer festivals in the spring and autumn, often featuring Welsh beers.

2019
Vale of Glamorgan's Most Improved Pub of the Year
Boathouse, Sully

Now in private ownership, the former Sully Inn has undergone extensive refurbishment whilst retaining the multi room. split level layout of old, wooden floors, original bar counter and a bar front made of reclaimed wood from an old Thai fishing boat.

2019
Bridgend's Most Improved Pub of the Year
Brittania, Bridgend

Formerly keg-only, the Britannia now regularly serves one ale and has installed a second for further use.

2019
Vale of Glamorgan and Bridgend's Community Pub of the Year
Mountain Hare, Brynnau Gwynion

A traditional Welsh village local, the pub has been in the same family for over forty years and boasts a traditional public bar and a lovely old stone walled lounge. One of the handpumps usually has one of the pub's own brews.

2019
Vale of Glamorgan's Community Pub of the Year
White Hart, Llantwit Major

Rewarded for the licencee's participation in the town's events, the pub is set in the picturesque town square, and dates back to the 15th Century. A cosy public bar with a large log burner, a separate restaurant, and a large beer garden at the back which hosts popular music events and beer festivals.

2019

Vale of Glamorgan and Bridgend "Highly Commended" Pub of the Year

Highly Recommended
The Golden Lion, Penarth

VoG and Bridgend CAMRA members voted for the Lion primarily for the superb quality of the three or four well-chosen ales from Welsh breweries, but also because it's a genuine pub, with layout and design that just makes it great to drink there.

Highly Recommended
The Pilot, Penarth

Operated with emphasis on fine food, with interesting and high quality ales. Seating outside the front for warm weather, and a view across Cardiff Bay to the back.

Highly Recommended
Coach Inn, Bridgend

A staggering array of pumpclips pay testament to the enthusiasm of the proprietors (and customers) for good quality real ale and cider. Well supported beer festivals.
